PEM water electrolysis, also known as proton exchange membrane (PEM) electrolysis, is a method of splitting water into its constituent elements, hydrogen and oxygen, using an electrochemical process. It involves the use of a proton exchange membrane as the electrolyte, which selectively allows the passage of protons (H+ ions) while blocking the flow of electrons.
